Where their first words matter.

A Day in Pre-K

Predictable rhythm, room to play.

Young children thrive on routine. Days at Renaissance Pre-K follow a consistent flow - so children know what comes next, settle in quickly, and feel safe to explore.

8:00 AM
Arrival & soft startGreetings, putting away bags, settling in with quiet centers
8:30
Morning circleDu'a, calendar, weather, helper of the day, and a short story
9:00
Qur'an & Arabic timeShort surahs, du'as, and Arabic letter recognition through songs & movement
9:45
Snack & wudu practiceBuilding independence with handwashing, snack manners, and the basics of wudu
10:15
Outdoor playGross motor skills, fresh air, and unstructured time with friends
11:00
Learning centersLiteracy, math, art, sensory, and dramatic play - rotating each week
12:00 PM
Lunch & Dhuhr timeFamily-style lunch, then nap mats out for rest

2:30
Special: art, music, or movementOne enrichment block per day
3:00
Closing circle & dismissalRecap the day, sing a closing du'a, see families at pickup
Curriculum

What we teach.

Three intertwined strands - academic readiness, faith formation, and character - woven through every part of the day rather than separated into competing blocks.

Early academics

  • Letter and sound recognition
  • Counting and number sense to 20
  • Pre-writing skills and pencil grip
  • Story comprehension and rich vocabulary
  • Color, shape, pattern, and sequencing

Qur'an & faith

  • Short surahs from Juz 'Amma by repetition
  • Daily du'as: eating, sleeping, traveling, wudu
  • Names of Allah, stories of the prophets
  • Arabic letters and high-frequency words
  • Wudu practice and the beginnings of salah

Character & community

  • Sharing, turn-taking, and friendship skills
  • Saying salaam, "alhamdulillah," and "thank you"
  • Helping in the classroom - small jobs daily
  • Naming and managing big feelings
  • Adab: respect for teachers, parents, friends
