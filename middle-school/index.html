Curriculum

What students learn.

A challenging program designed so that students enter high school ready for AP coursework - and ready for the next stage of their Islamic identity.

Academic core

  • Pre-Algebra, Algebra I, Geometry
  • ELA - literature, composition, public speaking
  • Science - Life, Earth, and Physical Science
  • Social Studies - World, U.S., Islamic history
  • Pre-AP and honors tracks for qualifying students

Qur'an, Arabic & Deen

  • Daily Qur'an - memorization & tajweed
  • Arabic literature and advanced grammar
  • Fiqh of worship, transactions, and family
  • Hadith - Forty Hadith of Nawawi and beyond
  • Seerah taught from primary sources

Electives & leadership

  • Robotics and computer science
  • Debate and public speaking
  • Visual arts and Islamic calligraphy
  • Student council and MSA leadership roles
  • Community service projects each semester
Advisory program

One trusted adult, three years long.

Every middle schooler is matched with an advisor in 6th grade and stays with that advisor through 8th. Advisory meets daily for check-ins, weekly for deeper conversations on identity, academics, and adab. It's the relationship that catches things teachers miss - and the reason middle school here feels small even as it gets more rigorous.
