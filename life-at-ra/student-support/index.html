Set up to succeed

Support before a student has to ask.

Some students need extra academic support, some need help navigating a hard week, and most need both at some point. Our small class sizes mean a struggling student is noticed early - not after it's become a pattern.

Areas of Support

Two kinds of support, working together.

Academic

Academic Support

Teachers flag struggling students early, with extra help built into the school week rather than left entirely to outside tutoring.

Social-Emotional

Social-Emotional Support

Staff trained to notice when a student is struggling socially or emotionally, with a clear path to check in and involve the family.

For Families

How to ask for help.

No wrong door

If you're not sure who to talk to about a concern, you don't need to figure it out before reaching out - contact the school and we'll route you to the right person, whether that's a classroom teacher, a grade-level lead, or school administration.
