The Process

Six steps to enrollment.

There's no part of this you have to figure out alone — an admissions team member is with you from your first message through orientation.

  1. Inquire

    Send us a quick note with your child's name, current grade, and what you're looking for. We'll respond within one business day with next steps and answer any early questions about fit, schedule, or program.

    Estimated time: 5 minutes

  2. Visit campus

    Tour the school during a regular school day. You'll see real classrooms, meet teachers, and observe how Qur'an, Arabic, and academics fit together — not a staged open house. Bring your child; we love when they come along.

    Tuesdays & Thursdays, by appointment

  3. Complete the application

    Submit the family application along with the application fee. The form covers basic family information, your child's school history, and a few open questions about your goals for them.

    Estimated time: 25 minutes

  4. Send supporting records

    Forward immunization records, the birth certificate, and — for grades 1 and up — the two most recent report cards, any standardized test results, and a teacher recommendation. Full checklist below.

    See documents checklist

  5. Family conversation

    Once the file is complete, you'll meet with the admissions team and a member of school leadership. We'll talk about fit on both sides — your hopes for your child, and how we can support them academically and spiritually.

    About 45 minutes

  6. Decision & enrollment

    We aim to send a decision within two weeks of a complete file. Once admitted, you'll secure your seat with the enrollment deposit and join us for new-family orientation before the first day.

    Decision in ~2 weeks

Timeline

How the cycle flows.

Renaissance reviews applications on a rolling basis — there's no single deadline. Seats are limited per grade, so earlier is better. Priority review for the next school year opens in early winter.

Step 1

Inquire

Open all year. Most families reach out 2–4 months before their target start date.

Step 2

Tour & apply

Schedule a tour within a week or two. Submit the application alongside or after.

Step 3

Records review

We review the full file within two weeks of receiving every document.

Step 4

Family conversation

A 45-minute meeting with the admissions team and school leadership.

Step 5

Decision & deposit

Admitted families secure their seat with the enrollment deposit.

Step 6

Orientation & first day

New-family orientation in August, followed by the first day of school.

Common Questions

About applying, specifically.

Can my child start mid-year?
Yes, if there's a seat in your child's grade. Mid-year transfers follow the exact process above — inquiry, tour, application, records, family conversation — and we can usually move quickly once the file is complete. Reach out and we'll tell you honestly what's open.
What if a document is missing or delayed?
Tell us as soon as you notice — schools and prior records offices can be slow, and we'd rather plan around it than have your application stall. We can often start the family conversation while a record is still in transit.
Is there an entrance exam or interview for my child?
No formal entrance exam. For older grades, the family conversation may include a short, informal check-in with your child — more conversation than test — so teachers can plan for the right level of support from day one.
What does the application fee cover, and is it refundable?
The application fee covers the cost of reviewing and processing your family's file and is separate from tuition. Like most schools, it's non-refundable regardless of the admissions decision. Ask the office for the current amount.
