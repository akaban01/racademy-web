Curriculum

What students learn.

A full Texas Foundation High School Program - paired with the most rigorous Islamic Studies program of any college-prep high school in the area.

Core academics

  • 4 years English (through AP Language & Literature)
  • 4 years Math (through AP Calculus)
  • 4 years Science (Bio, Chem, Physics, AP options)
  • 4 years Social Studies (with AP options)
  • Texas endorsements: STEM, Arts & Humanities, Business

Qur'an & Islamic Studies

  • Daily Qur'an: memorization tracks & tafseer
  • Aqeedah, fiqh, and usul al-fiqh
  • Hadith sciences and seerah
  • Contemporary Islamic thought & ethics
  • Arabic literature - classical and modern

Electives & leadership

  • Athletics: basketball, soccer, track
  • Debate, journalism, robotics, computer science
  • Visual arts and Islamic calligraphy
  • MSA leadership and student government
  • Community service and internships

College Counseling

From sophomore year on.

Our college counselor begins working with families in 10th grade - building a transcript that reflects each student's strengths, identifying the right schools, and walking through every application step.

Where graduates go.

Renaissance graduates have matriculated to the University of Texas at Austin, Texas A&M, UT Dallas, UT San Antonio, Baylor, Rice, and several out-of-state and international programs - pursuing engineering, medicine, business, computer science, and Islamic studies.

Senior capstone

Every senior writes a thesis.

In their final year, every Renaissance student completes a capstone project - a research thesis or original work synthesizing their academic and Islamic learning. Past topics have ranged from environmental ethics in fiqh to AI bias to community-based public health. It's the project they'll be proudest of when they leave.
