Pillar 2

Character, taught like a subject.

Tarbiyah means upbringing - the deliberate cultivation of a person's character, habits, and relationship with Allah. At Renaissance it isn't an assembly or a once-a-week add-on; it's woven into the rhythm of every single school day.

A Day's Rhythm

Tarbiyah throughout the school day.

The same care given to math or reading is given to a student's character and connection to faith - built into the schedule, not squeezed around it.

Morning

Du'a & assembly

The school day opens together - morning du'a, a few words of reminder, and a shared sense of starting the day with intention.

Daily

Qur'an period

Every grade has dedicated, daily Qur'an instruction - recitation, memorization, and understanding, taught by a qualified Qur'an teacher.

Midday

Prayer in jama'ah

Students pray Dhuhr together on campus, learning the rhythm of communal prayer as a normal part of the school day, not an exception to it.

Woven In

Akhlaq & character

Lessons on manners, honesty, and responsibility show up inside everyday classroom moments - corrected and reinforced the way any other habit is taught.

Closing

Reflection

The day closes with a moment to reflect - a small habit that adds up to a student who's used to checking in with themselves.

Our Approach

Formation, not just information.

Why it matters

Knowing what's right and doing what's right are two different skills, and the second one takes repetition. We treat tarbiyah the same way we treat any other subject that needs daily practice to stick - small, consistent habits reinforced by every teacher, every day, until they become who a student is rather than something the student was once taught.

What It Looks Like

Tarbiyah in practice.

✓

Daily Qur'an instruction at every grade level, from introduction to memorization.

✓

Communal prayer built into the school schedule, not left for home.

✓

Islamic studies taught as its own subject, with its own standards and progression.

✓

Manners and conduct addressed directly by teachers, in the moment, all day long.

✓

Older students mentoring younger ones, practicing leadership and responsibility.

✓

Family partnership - what's taught on campus is reinforced, not contradicted, at home.
